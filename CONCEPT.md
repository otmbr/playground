# LOW NOISE

> A playable sound ritual for a world that is too loud.
>
> *Play less. Feel more. — Music from below the noise.*

LOW NOISE is an interactive music game where you don't get **louder** — you make
the world **quieter**.

It is not a music app.
It is not a classic game.
It is not a meditation tool.

It is a **futuristic noise ritual** for the smartphone.

You open the app, put on headphones, and press **REDUCE**. A 90-second run
begins.

The app does the opposite of a normal rhythm game. Instead of giving you music
to tap along to, **your environment becomes the raw material**. Street, wind,
voices, footsteps, room tone, your breath — everything is translated *locally*
into abstract music. Not as a recording, but as a **noise fingerprint**. From
that, a living sound space emerges.

You don't play against enemies. **You play against disturbance.**

---

## The Core Idea

LOW NOISE is a game about **focus**.

The screen shows no character, no buttons, no level map. Instead you see a dark,
fluid soundscape:

- Black space
- Neon lines
- Bass waves
- Interference particles
- A trembling signal in the center

Your task: **Keep the signal clean.**

But not with classic game controls. You play with **movement, touch, stillness,
breath, and environment**.

---

## The Core Loop

### 1. SCAN
The app briefly listens to the environment. Not creepy, not as a recording —
only as abstract analysis:

`Noise Level · Pulse · Density · Sharpness · Human Presence · Low Frequency`

From this it generates your current state:

```
NOISEPRINT: 72% HUMAN / 18% WIND / 10% MACHINE
```

That is your starting point.

### 2. DESCEND
You sink into a visual sound tunnel. At first everything is noisy. The beat is
barely audible. The bass is buried. You must **excavate** the sound — not
through frantic tapping, but through precise, calm interaction.

### 3. FILTER
You move the phone gently:

| Gesture | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Tilt left / right | Shifts the filter |
| Tilt forward / back | Changes the depth |
| Hold finger | Stabilizes the signal |
| Slow swipe | Pulls interference lines out of the sound |
| Short tap | Removes digital artifacts |
| Two fingers | Opens dub echo |
| Stay still | Charges the bass |

The new idea: **stillness is an action**. Not always *more* — but *less, more
precise, deeper*.

### 4. DROP INTO LOW
When you keep the signal clean long enough, there is no classic drop. There is a
**descent**:

- The screen darkens
- The highs disappear
- The sub comes in
- Everything becomes slower, heavier, warmer

Then it appears:

```
LOW FOUND
```

From here you no longer play *against* the noise — you play *with* it. This is
the magic moment.

## What Is New About It

Most music games ask: *Hit the beat.*

LOW NOISE asks: **Can you free the beat from the world?**

It is not a rhythm game. It is a **noise-shaping game**.

- The environment is different every time.
- Your state is different every time.
- Your loop is different every time.

On a train it sounds different than at the sea. La Spezia sounds different than
Frankfurt. A quiet room sounds different than a train station. The game doesn't
get *bigger* — it gets **more personal**.

---

## The Mechanics

- **Noise** — Everything begins chaotically: too much movement, too many
  interference frequencies, too much screen activity.
- **Signal** — Through calm gestures you pull a clear core out of the chaos.
- **Bass** — The more stable you become, the deeper the sound gets.
- **Bloom** — At the end, your personal loop emerges.

## The Modes

1. **LOW RUN** — The main mode. 90 seconds. A fast, intense noise run. Built for
   virality.
2. **DEEP ROOM** — Free mode. No goal, no end. You control filter, echo, bass and
   glitch like a minimalist instrument. For people who just want to stay.
3. **CITY DUB** — You walk through the city. The app reacts to movement and
   surroundings. Traffic lights, footsteps, traffic, voices — all become an
   abstract dub system. A *Walkman from the future*.
4. **NIGHT SIGNAL** — The darker mode. Slower. More bass. More ambient. More
   feeling.
5. **NOISE DUEL** — Two people start at the same time. Not whoever is louder
   wins — whoever holds the more stable signal. Both create a combined
   **SHARED SIGNAL** loop at the end. Brutally good for social.

## Why It Beats a Normal Music Game

Because it has a real **stance**. A normal music game says *do more*. LOW NOISE
says **do less — but right**. That fits today perfectly: every app wants
attention; this app turns attention into music. It is almost an **anti-app** —
yet still sexy, futuristic, shareable, interactive, brandable.

---

## MVP

The first version does not need to be big. It needs only:

- **One screen** — a dark frequency space.
- **One mode** — 90-second LOW RUN.
- **Five interactions** — Tilt, Hold, Swipe, Tap, Stillness.
- **One sound set** — Kick, Sub, Pad, Noise, Echo, Glitch.
- **One result** — shareable loop + Noise Report.

That's enough. If the first run feels good, the thing is strong.
