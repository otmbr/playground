The Science

Theoretical

What is a gravastar?

A gravatational vacuum star is a hypothetical ultra-compact object: from far away almost indistinguishable from a black hole, but without a classical singularity or event horizon. Its interior is filled with a dark-energy-like vacuum whose outward pressure balances gravity.

Theoretical · 2026

The Frankfurt formation idea

Daniel Jampolski and Luciano Rezzolla (Goethe University Frankfurt, 2026) describe — within Einstein's General Relativity — a way a gravastar could form dynamically during collapse: a de Sitter-like region nucleates at the centre, starting at effectively zero size and expanding outward like a mini-universe. The expansion slows near the Schwarzschild radius and can meet the infalling matter in a static equilibrium — a shell. Under other conditions, collapse to a black hole stays inevitable.

Accepted

Compactness & the three fates

Compactness is the ratio C = rs / 2R of the Schwarzschild radius to the object's size. The closer C approaches 0.5, the nearer the surface sits to forming a horizon. This app reads C live and resolves one of three outcomes:

  • Stable star — gravity and pressure balance (low C).
  • Black hole — gravity wins; a horizon & photon ring form.
  • Gravastar — vacuum pressure halts collapse; a shell + expanding interior appear.

What the dials mean

  • Mass — sets the Schwarzschild radius (real GR).
  • Radius — how compressed the star is → compactness.
  • Vacuum Energy — dark-energy-like outward push of the interior.
  • Shell Tension — stability of the balance boundary.
  • Quantum Noise — instability / uncertainty near the limit.
  • Entropy Leakage — how information behaves at the boundary.

The outcome thresholds here are artistic toy-model values inspired by the literature — not physical predictions.
